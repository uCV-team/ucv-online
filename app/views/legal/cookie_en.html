Cookie Policy

Last Updated: May 1, 2020

PubliCV does not use any tracking technology. The only data we know is the one freely provided by you along with your IP address.

Most importantly, we don’t employ tracking cookies.

We only employ technical cookies and the Google Analytics anonymized cookie.

According to European Law, we don’t use any technology for which consent has to be preventively granted.

A list with all the cookie-related technologies and their finality is hereby provided.

What technologies are used and how/why?

Cookies

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device as you are browsing. They are processed and stored by your web browser. In and of themselves, cookies are harmless and serve crucial functions for websites. Cookies can also generally be easily viewed and deleted.

We only use:

  • Strictly necessary cookies  --  These cookies are essential for you to browse the website and use its features, such as accessing secure areas of the site. Cookies that allow web shops to hold your items in your cart while you are shopping online are an example of strictly necessary cookies. These cookies will generally be first-party session cookies. While it is not required to obtain consent for these cookies, what they do, and why they are necessary, should be explained to the user.

    On PubliCV, strictly necessary cookies are used to handle your session after you sign up or log in. This functionality is needed to tell you from other users. Hence, they are used to grant the owner of the concerning content/data some functionalities such as editing and destroying the content/data. Otherwise, everyone could edit your profile, and that is something we actively prevent.

Further information about cookies is available at https://gdpr.eu/cookies/

IP Address

An Internet Protocol address  ( IP address ) is a numerical label assigned to each device connected to a computer network  that uses the Internet Protocol  for communication.   An IP address serves two main functions: host or network interface identification  and location addressing .

We may save your IP address on our logs and database in order to identify abuses (for instance terms violations). We also use it to center the map on the homepage based on your approximate location. However, we don’t ever save nor track your location. Also, consider that the location that we can infer through your IP address is very approximate. We may be able to guess your province but not the actual city you’re in and, for sure, not your current address.
